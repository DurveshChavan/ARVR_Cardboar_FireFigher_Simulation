# User Guide — VR Firefighter Training Game

## Equipment Needed

- 📱 Android smartphone (your device)
- 🥽 Google Cardboard VR headset
- 🎮 Bluetooth Xbox controller (paired to phone)

---

## Setup

### 1. Pair your Xbox Controller
1. Hold the **Xbox button** to turn on the controller
2. Hold the **Pair button** (small button on top) until the Xbox logo flashes fast
3. On your phone: Settings → Bluetooth → pair **Xbox Wireless Controller**

### 2. Insert phone into Cardboard
- Hold phone in **landscape** (horizontal)
- Insert with the **volume buttons pointing UP**
- Snap the headset closed

### 3. Launch the app
- The screen should show two cards side-by-side (one for each eye)
- Hold the headset up and look forward — the view will auto-center after ~1 second

---

## How to Play

### Lobby Screen

You'll see two scenario cards:

| Card | Button | Scenario |
|---|---|---|
| 🔥 Kitchen Fire | **Press A** | Gas fire in a kitchen |
| 🖥️ Server Room Fire | **Press B** | Electrical fire in server racks |

Press **A** or **B** on your Xbox controller to start.

---

### Controls

```
Left Stick  ──→  Walk around
Right Stick ──→  Look left/right/up/down
Y Button    ──→  Switch extinguisher type
LT          ──→  Aim the extinguisher
RT          ──→  Spray / fire
```

**Moving head:** The gyroscope tracks your real head movement automatically — just turn your head inside the headset!

---

### Selecting the Right Extinguisher

Press **Y** to cycle between extinguisher types. The model in your hand changes:

| Label | Color | Use For |
|---|---|---|
| **DCP** (Dry Chemical Powder) | Red + black | Kitchen gas fires ✅ |
| **CO2** | Grey / silver | Server room electrical fires ✅ |
| **Water** | Blue | ❌ Never use on gas or electrical fires |

> ⚠️ **Using Water on an electrical fire = instant failure and "Electrocution Hazard" warning!**

---

### Extinguishing the Fire

1. **Walk toward the fire** (left stick)
2. **Aim at the fire** (look + right stick)
3. **Hold LT** to raise the extinguisher into aiming position
4. **Hold RT** to spray

The fire will shrink as you spray. When all fires are out:

```
✅ MISSION COMPLETE!
   Fire suppressed.
```

---

### Time Limit

You have **60 seconds** to extinguish all fires. The timer shows in green text at the top of your view.

If time runs out:
```
❌ MISSION FAILED
   Time expired!
```

---

### Returning to Lobby

After any result (win or fail), the screen shows:

```
[Hold A to return to lobby]
```

Hold the **A button for 1.5 seconds** — a percentage indicator shows progress. Release before 1.5 seconds to cancel and stay on the result screen.

## Scenario Briefings

### 🔥 Kitchen Fire

**Situation:** A gas hob fire has ignited in the commercial kitchen.

**Correct extinguisher:** 🔴 **DCP (Dry Chemical Powder)**
- Dry powder smothers gas fires by interrupting the combustion reaction
- Works on Class A, B, and C fires

**Why NOT water?** Water on a burning gas jet can splash and spread the fire.

**Why NOT CO2?** CO2 is less effective on open gas flames and provides no residual protection.

---

### 🖥️ Server Room Fire

**Situation:** Three server racks in the data center are on fire due to an electrical fault.

**Correct extinguisher:** ⬛ **CO2 (Carbon Dioxide)**
- CO2 displaces oxygen without leaving residue that damages equipment
- Safe for electrical fires — non-conductive

**Why NOT water?** Water conducts electricity → **electrocution risk** → INSTANT FAIL

**Why NOT DCP?** Powder will destroy all server equipment (acceptable in emergency but bad practice — and slower than CO2 here).

**Note:** All three rack fires must be suppressed to win.

---

## Fire Safety — Real World Reference

| Fire Class | Type | Correct Extinguisher |
|---|---|---|
| Class A | Ordinary combustibles (wood, paper) | Water, DCP, CO2 |
| Class B | Flammable liquids/gases | DCP, CO2, Foam |
| Class C | Electrical equipment | CO2 only |
| Class D | Metal fires | Dry powder (specialist) |
| Class F | Cooking oils | Wet chemical |

> Always follow your organization's specific fire safety procedures and evacuation protocols. This game is a training aid only.
